# EMBERFALL — World Architecture

> The deep-world expansion of `STORY.md` (which remains canon — this document
> widens it from 5 zones to a continent). Built phase-by-phase with the World
> Architect brief (2026-07-17). Phase 1 = the core. Later phases: power &
> peoples, the 12-zone map, depth & hooks.

## CONFIG (chosen, per brief)

- **Genre:** dark fantasy × mythpunk — a frontier world running on a wounded,
  half-divine fire; folklore is infrastructure.
- **Tone:** hopeful but scarred. Cozy hearths, cold wilds; kindness is a
  survival strategy, not a mood.
- **Touchstones (3):** Elden Ring's beautiful decay · WoW's faction identity ·
  Morrowind's alien-but-coherent cultures.
- **Hard NOs:** prophecy plots, chosen ones, generic undead hordes, standard
  elves/dwarves/orcs, "ancient evil awakens," amnesia openings.
- **Scope:** the continent of Vesper — 12 zones, 3 playable factions, launch
  content + 2 sealed expansion doors.

# PHASE 1 — THE CORE

## 1. High concept

**Every fire in Vesper is borrowed, and the lender is dying.** The world runs
on the Ember — a living fire buried wounded beneath the continent — and every
forge lit, spell cast, harvest quickened, and dead adventurer rekindled at a
hearth is an overdraft drawn against a creature that can *feel* the
withdrawal. Civilization is not a kingdom; it is a **ledger of heat**, and
the world has been running a deficit for a hundred years. No other MMO makes
its economy literally theological: in Emberfall, the account books balance
because the alternative is the last winter.

## 2. The Wound: the Emberfall (≈100 years ago)

**What actually happened** (unchanged canon, now sharpened): the Accord's
binding-keepers, the **Lampwrights** *(naming: Accord guild-compound, plain
craft-speech — "those who wright lamps")*, attempted to bind the entire Ember
into a single governed core — an everlasting flame, an end to hard winters
forever. The Ember was not a force; it was a **being**. The binding was a
cage, and the creature convulsed. It shattered upward through the sky in a
night of burning rain, wounded itself half to death, and buried its heart
deep beneath what is now the Cinder Depths, where it has been bleeding
shard-fire into the world ever since — half-dreaming, never healing.

**Three accounts, none complete** — the gap between them *is* Vesperan
politics:

- **The Grate-Debt** *(Hearthlands folk account; "grate" as in fireplace —
  homestead plain-speech)*: the old kingdoms simply spent more warmth than the
  world had. No villain, no crime — a bankruptcy. Moral: live small, bank your
  coals, never borrow what you can't feed back. This is why Hearthwardens
  distrust ambition itself — and why they'd rather bury a warm relic than
  study it.
- **The Great Accident** *(Lantern Order's taught history; "Order" naming:
  Accord institutional Latinate)*: a noble experiment — the Everlasting Lamp —
  failed at the last hour through impurity in the vessel, sabotage, or error.
  Knowledge was not the sin; sloppiness was. Moral: recover the records, find
  the flaw, and civilization can be re-lit *correctly*. **The Order's inner
  circle knows this account is a lie** — the experiment didn't fail; it
  *worked*, briefly, on something alive.
- **The Wounding** *(Cinderborn account; their names are always injuries and
  mendings)*: the fire was a living thing that the Accord tried to cage, and
  it did what any animal does in a trap — it broke its own body to get out.
  It is not gone. It is under the mountain, hurt and dreaming, and the whole
  continent is walking on its sickbed. Moral: stop *taking*. Start *tending*.
  Dismissed by the towns as scar-madness — it is the closest account to true.

## 3. Cosmology

**Creation — the First Hearth.** Before anything, cold. Then one fire, no
teller says lit by whom, and the world is its hearth: stone is banked ash,
seas are quench-water, and every living thing carries a **spark** — a coal
lent from the First Hearth's bed. The Ember is not a god; it is the *remnant*
of that original fire, grown intimate with the world it warmed. Vesperans
don't pray. They **tend** — because you don't worship a fire, you keep it.

**Death — the flue.** When a body dies its spark comes loose and falls *down*
— Vesperans say it "goes down the flue" — drawn back toward the Ember to
rejoin the warm. That is the whole folk afterlife: *going home to the warm.*
It is why graves in Vesper are shallow and hearths are sacred, and why the
worst oath in the language is to wish someone a **cold falling** — a death so
far from any hearth that the spark gutters before it gets home. The deep
ember-scars are full of what that produces: the **Unreturned** — sparks that
went out mid-fall and rekindled *wrong*, lantern-pale, looking for a warmth
they no longer remember the shape of. (Vesper's answer to undead: not cursed
corpses — lost heat.)

**Resurrection — the Hearth-Share (the loophole that makes respawn canon).**
A tended hearth can hold a **share** of a living spark — a splinter of your
coal, banked in the communal fire, renewed every time you rest beside it.
Die anywhere the hearth can reach, and it *pulls*: your spark is hauled back
up the flue and rekindled around the banked share. This is the oldest craft
in Vesper, older than the Accord, and it is why:

- **Respawn** = your hearth-share rekindling you at the last fire you tended.
- **Death durability** = the rekindling draws its toll of heat through what
  you carry — gear comes back scorched (15 durability, already in game, now
  canon).
- **Dungeon ejection** = instanced deep-places are beyond hearth-reach; the
  wardens' fire can only drag you back to the gate ("the wardens dragged you
  back outside" — the live line, now doctrine).
- **Fast travel** = **waystones** are surviving junctions of the Accord's
  lamp-line; travelers ride the old conduits *as heat*, and the fee pays the
  stone's tender.
- **Monster repopulation** = the wilds sit on the Ember's open wound;
  shard-fire re-kindles what dies out there within hours. Kill an emberling
  and the scar simply *exhales another*. (Bandits repopulate the ordinary
  way: desperation.)

**The stakes — the Long Cooling.** Since the Fall, every winter in living
memory has been a little longer than the last. The Ember warmed Vesper the
way marrow warms a body; a wounded Ember warms it less each year. Nobody
official says the phrase out loud, but every farmer knows it: *the world is
cooling.*

## 4. The Engine of Conflict: **cinder**

Shard-fire cools where it lands into a workable, burnable, *living* residue —
**cinder** *(folk plain-speech; nobody dignifies it with a grander name
because everybody needs it)*. It is the only fuel that still burns hot enough
to matter in a cooling world, and it is:

- **Harvestable** — mined and gathered in ember-scarred ground; the deeper
  the scar, the richer the seam. (Mechanically: the existing gathering loop,
  and a future cinder-fuel resource.)
- **Contestable** — the richest scars are exactly the most dangerous ground.
  The Ashreach *is* a cinder rush: warlords hold veins the way old wars held
  rivers. Every faction flashpoint in Phase 3 is ultimately a cinder claim.
- **Corruptible** — cinder is the Ember's blood, and blood remembers. Carry
  too much too long, sleep too close to a seam, and you begin to **kindle**:
  faint light under the skin, warmth that isn't yours, dreams that aren't
  either. That is what the Cinderborn *are*. It is also what emberlings,
  ash-wolves, and the twisted wilds are — kindling without a mind to steward
  it.

**Why it cannot be resolved:** the towns must burn cinder or freeze (the
Hearthwardens' unhappy arithmetic); the Order must spend it to wake relics
and buy back knowledge (their unhappy arithmetic); the Lampwrights hoard it
because a second, "correct" binding will take more than the first; and the
Cinderborn watch all three mine the blood of a living thing and call the
extraction by its true name: flaying. Every side is right about its need.
No side can stop needing. **That arithmetic — heat in, heat out, and a
wounded lender under the floor — is the war.**

---

### Phase 1 — decisions made (3)

1. **Anchored to shipped canon.** STORY.md's Ember, Lampwrights, factions,
   and the Cinder Depths climax all stand; the architecture deepens rather
   than replaces, so every existing quest, zone, and NPC stays true.
2. **Made the economy the theology.** "Ledger of heat" turns the game's
   actual ledger rule, coin sinks, durability tolls, and skilling loops into
   worldview — systems and lore now say the same sentence.
3. **Chose cooling over corruption as the doom-clock.** The existential
   pressure is a lengthening winter (slow, civic, arguable) rather than
   spreading evil terrain — it forces politics, not just combat.

### Alternatives rejected (2)

1. **A clean-slate world** (new continent, new cataclysm) — maximum novelty,
   but it would orphan a live game's story spine and retcon quests players
   have already done. Rejected: continuity is worth more than a blank page.
2. **Cinder as literal currency** (replace coins) — thematically pure, but it
   would demolish a tuned, ledgered economy and conflate fuel with money.
   Rejected in favor of cinder as a *resource* layered beside coins.

# PHASE 2 — POWER & PEOPLE

> Player direction incorporated (2026-07-17): multiple, much bigger skill
> trees; many more attacks than Strike/Power Strike/Mend; classes (warrior/
> ranger/mage plus others); weapon types with distinct attacks. The GDD's
> "classless" lock is REOPENED by explicit user request and resolved as a
> hybrid: **classless skills underneath, class identity on top** (Callings).

## 1. The power system: Kindling

**Source.** All power is **kindling** — drawing heat through your own spark
(the coal every living thing carries on loan from the First Hearth). There
is no mana and no gods' favor; there is only heat, and heat always comes
from somewhere: your body's warmth, the cinder in your pack, or the banked
share in a hearth. Magic in Vesper is *accounting with your life*.

**Hard rules (and their mechanical faces):**
1. **Everything drawn is owed.** Energy is body-heat; it regenerates because
   you burn food and rest by fires. (Existing energy/regen = canon.)
2. **A spark channels only so fast.** Draw faster than the fire breathes and
   it gutters — the global cooldown is called **the Breath**, and every
   discipline lives inside it. (The ~1.5s GCD = canon.)
3. **Heat dissipates.** Range is real: thrown heat weakens with distance;
   the archer's answer is to make the *arrow* carry it.
4. **Overdraw grays you.** Push past your spark's rate and cold creeps in —
   numb fingers, frost-scars, slowed blood (mechanical hooks: burnout
   debuffs for future big-spender abilities).
5. **The Ember feels deep draws.** Kindle hard in scarred land and things
   wake and come (invasions, dungeon adds, future scar events = canon).
6. **What you kindle, you must steward.** Heat given a shape and abandoned
   becomes wild — Vesper's monsters in miniature. This is the law the
   forbidden discipline breaks.

## 2. Callings — six class fantasies on one classless chassis

Your **skills** stay classless (train anything, RS-style). Your **Calling**
is a chosen discipline that opens its **tree** — a big, branching perk tree
in the fantasy of the class — plus its ability kit. Weapons carry their own
attacks; Calling + weapon = your build. Retraining a Calling is a costly
respec, not a reroll (identity with freedom).

Launch skills grow 6 → 8: **Melee, Ranged, Magic, Vitality** (combat) +
Mining, Fishing, Smithing, Cooking (life) — fulfilling the GDD's original
"8 launch skills."

| Calling | Fantasy | Primary skill | Weapons | Kit feel (examples) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Warden** *(from Hearthwarden plain-speech)* | shield-wall tank | Melee | sword+shield, maul | Bulwark (block-stance), Shield Slam (stagger), Hold the Line (taunt aura), Banked Heat (absorb→retaliate) |
| **Reaver** *(frontier raider-speech)* | berserker | Melee | axe, greatsword | Cleave (arc hit), Bloodfire (spend HP for damage), Overdraw (haste that grays you after), Executioner's Arc |
| **Strider** *(Hearthlands hunter-speech)* | ranger | Ranged | shortbow, longbow | Aimed Shot (windup), Pinning Shot (root), Ember-tipped Volley, Hunter's Mark (party damage bonus) |
| **Cinderwright** *(Accord craft-compound, like Lampwright)* | fire mage | Magic | staff, focus | Cinderbolt (spammable), Kindle Ward (absorb shield), Scorchwave (AoE), Ignite (burn DoT) |
| **Hearthmender** *(hearth-share craft turned outward)* | healer/support | Magic | focus, staff | Mend (canon — becomes their opener), Warmth (HoT aura), Rekindle (combat res, long cooldown), Share the Fire (transfer energy) |
| **Ashwalker** *(Cinderborn road-name)* | rogue/skirmisher | Melee | daggers | Quench (brief stealth — you *cool* out of sight), Cold Read (guaranteed crit from stealth), Ash Veil (dodge stance), Warmth-Seeker (bonus vs burning targets) |

**Weapon types carry attacks** (weapon = moveset, Calling = tree): swords
balanced with a parry; axes slow with bleeds; mauls stagger; daggers fast
with crit windows; bows add range and windup shots; staves/focuses channel
kindled bolts. Existing Strike/Power Strike fold in as the universal
unarmed/basic sword kit — nothing shipped is orphaned.

**Trees, sized properly.** Each Calling gets a full tree (~15–20 nodes in
three branches, e.g. Warden: Bastion / Retribution / Watchfire), replacing
"3 pairs of perks" as the endgame of character identity. The current melee
perk tiers become the shared **Fighter's Trunk** all melee Callings inherit
— shipped picks stay valid.

**The forbidden discipline: Lampwrighting (Binding).** The seventh craft
leashes *living* heat — dominating kindled creatures, siphoning another
person's spark, banking stolen warmth in phylactery-lamps. It is forbidden
by every people for the same reason: it treats someone else's coal as fuel.
**Someone is practicing it now:** a cell inside the Lantern Order's Archive
(the Order's inner circle suspects, and dares not audit itself). Raid-tier
antagonist kit; a future corrupted-questline temptation for players.

## 3. Four playable peoples (no monocultures — every people gets dissidents)

1. **Hearthfolk** *(plain-speech)* — the heartland settlers. Origin: the
   Accord's farm provinces, the ones who stayed and rebuilt. Values: tend
   the fire, feed the guest, pay the debt. Look: quilted wool, copper
   charms, soot-tattoo tally marks on the forearm for winters survived.
   Schism: **Banked** (wall the towns, ration, endure) vs **Outburners**
   (push the frontier while there's still warmth to spend). Darkest act:
   **the Quenching at Marrowgate** — the first winter after the Fall, a
   column of kindled refugees was barred from the gates and left to the
   cold; the tally-scarred survivors' descendants still won't say the town's
   old name.
2. **Cinderborn** *(their names are injuries and mendings)* — the
   scar-changed. Origin: people the shard-fire remade instead of killing.
   Values: tend-don't-take, witness everything, endure without forgetting.
   Look: under-skin glow at the collarbones, ash-gray palettes, unburnable
   hair worn long. Schism: **Menders** (quiet stewardship of the wound) vs
   the **Ashen Choir** (the Ember's pain must be *heard* — let it bleed
   louder until Vesper listens). Darkest act: a Choir cell fed an entire
   town's hearth-shares into a scar-seam — forty true deaths, cold fallings
   all — "so they would finally hear it scream."
3. **The Vossari** *(salt-tongue; long vowels, doubled s)* — the
   quench-fleet. Origin: those who fled the Fall by sea and made the fleets
   home. Values: the wake behind you is paid for; salt over ash; never
   anchor twice. Look: quench-water tattoos that darken with age,
   whalebone toggles, oil-sealed braids. Schism: **Shoremade** (settle the
   coasts, trade inland) vs the **Never-Landed** (the land is a pyre that
   hasn't finished burning; keep the decks between you and it). Darkest
   act: **the Oar Wall** — during the Fall, the fleets beat burning
   refugee boats back from the anchorage at oar-point to keep the fire off
   the hulls. Their oldest funeral song has a verse nobody translates for
   outsiders.
4. **The Dolm** *(mountain forge-speech; one hard syllable)* — the
   deep-miners. Origin: pre-Accord mountain holds that survived the Fall
   underground. Values: the mountain remembers; debts are carved, not
   spoken; ask first, cut second (their mining rite — they know the seams
   are alive, and mine anyway, with apology). Look: broad-built, lamp-pale,
   ledger-braids threaded with tally-beads; every family door carved with
   its debts. Schism: **Rite-keepers** (no cut without the asking) vs the
   **Open-Vein** (the Cooling voids old courtesies — dig). Darkest act:
   **the Sealed Shift** — when a gallery fire threatened the hold, the
   doors were closed on three hundred of their own, alive; the names are
   carved on the inside of the doors, where only the mountain reads them.

## 4. Three factions (cross-cutting — each recruits from all four peoples)

1. **The Hearthwarden League** *(canon Hearthwardens, grown into a
   federation)* — *"A small warm world beats a great burning one."*
   **Right about:** modesty works — their towns are the only places that
   feel alive. **Wrong about:** you cannot ration your way out of a cooling
   world; the walls are a slow surrender. **Endgame:** the Thousand Hearths
   — a federated frontier where no one sleeps beyond hearth-reach.
   **Justified atrocity:** quench-quarantine — kindled families are
   cold-exiled beyond the hearth-lines "for the town's sake," which every
   Warden knows is a sentence of true death by another name.
2. **The Lantern Order** *(canon)* — *"Ignorance wearing ambition's coat
   broke the world; knowledge will relight it."* **Right about:** the
   Cooling cannot be out-rationed — only understanding the Ember saves
   everyone. **Wrong about:** they are re-walking the Lampwrights' road
   with better footnotes, and the inner circle's secret (the binding
   *worked*) is exactly the fact that would stop them. **Endgame:** the
   Second Lamp — a controlled, consensual re-lighting of civilization.
   **Justified atrocity:** the Archive Fires — every independent copy of
   pre-Fall binding lore burned, every unlicensed researcher bought,
   silenced, or lost at sea, to keep the dangerous knowledge in one pair of
   careful hands. Theirs.
3. **The Tendfast** *(plain compound: hold-fast-to-tending; rooted in
   Cinderborn Mender belief, now cross-people)* — *"The Ember is neither
   fuel nor god but a patient — and Vesper lives only if it heals."*
   **Right about:** the Ember is alive, and healing it would end the
   Cooling at the source. **Wrong about:** healing means giving heat
   *back*, and they have stopped asking whose; and a healed Ember may
   remember what Vesper did to it. **Endgame:** the Great Tending — a
   continent-wide return of banked warmth to the wound. **Justified
   atrocity:** hearth-tithes — siphoning a quiet fraction of every
   refusing town's hearth-shares; rekindlings fail a little more often
   there, and no one can prove why.

*(The Lampwrights' shadow remains above-faction: the antagonist thread all
three factions would unite against — if admitting it exists didn't indict
each of them.)*

### Phase 2 — decisions made (3)

1. **Classless chassis, Calling identity.** Skills stay trainable by anyone
   (the shipped system); classes arrive as chosen disciplines with big
   trees and kits. Respec-not-reroll keeps "one character, one reputation."
2. **Weapons carry movesets.** Attack variety scales with the armory, not
   just the tree — every new weapon type is new gameplay, which keeps
   loot exciting forever.
3. **Peoples are cultures, not stat-races.** All four are human-stock
   shaped by the Fall (the Cinderborn barely still) — identity is values
   and history, cosmetics and dialogue, never +2 strength.

### Alternatives rejected (2)

1. **Hard classes at character creation** (WoW-style) — cleaner fantasy
   onboarding, but it retcons every existing character and kills the
   RS-style "your account is your story" pillar. Rejected for Callings.
2. **A fourth playable faction (the Lampwrights redeemed)** — delicious,
   but it collapses the mystery of the shadow-thread and makes the
   forbidden discipline a starting option instead of a temptation.
   Rejected; kept as post-launch corruption arc material.
