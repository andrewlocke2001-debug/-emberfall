# EMBERFALL — Story Bible

> The narrative source of truth. Grounded in `design/GDD.md` (world, tone,
> zones, locked decisions). The GDD calls for "one Main Story spine (FFXIV)
> threading all zones" + ~25 quests, 5 spotlight; this file is that spine and
> the lore it hangs on. Tone: **low-fantasy frontier — cozy towns, dangerous
> wilds, danger always opt-in and signposted.** Keep it human-scale; the
> mystery is bigger than any one hero.

---

## Logline

A century after the night the sky burned, a nobody homesteader on the frontier
of a broken continent goes looking for a better life in the ruins — and
uncovers what really caused the Emberfall, and the choice that could undo it or
finish it.

## The world: Vesper

**Vesper** is a continent that used to be whole. Before the Fall it was held
together by the **Ember** — not a god and not magic exactly, but a living fire
that ran under the world like sap under bark: it warmed the winters, quickened
the harvests, and lit the great lamps of the old kingdoms (collectively
remembered as **the Accord**). People didn't worship the Ember. They *used* it.
They got very good at using it. That was the problem.

## The Emberfall (≈100 years ago)

One night the sky burned and shattered the old kingdoms. The Ember — which the
Accord had been drawing harder and harder to feed its cities — broke. It came
down as a rain of burning shards, and where the shards landed the land and its
creatures were changed: scorched, twisted, *kindled*. The Accord didn't fall to
an army; it fell to its own appetite, in a single night now called the
**Emberfall**.

What survived crawled out into a colder, meaner world. No central power, no
lamps, no Accord — just scattered settlements clinging to the green edges while
the **ember-scars** (lands soaked in shard-fire) spread inward toward the place
the heart of the Ember came down. A hundred years on, the burning has cooled to
embers, the wilds have grown back wrong, and the frontier is pushing outward
again — for farmland, for safety, and for the **relics**: pre-Fall artifacts,
some still warm with Ember, worth a kingdom or a life.

## What people believe vs. the truth

- **Common belief:** the Emberfall was a natural catastrophe — the Ember "got
  too hot," the sky "got sick." A disaster, not a crime. You don't poke it; you
  rebuild away from it.
- **The truth (revealed across the spine):** the Accord *engineered* the
  overdraw. A faction of its own keepers — the **Lampwrights** — tried to bind
  the entire Ember into a single core they could control forever (an
  everlasting flame, a kingdom that never has a hard winter again). The binding
  is what shattered it. The Ember didn't die in the Fall; it was **wounded and
  buried**, still smoldering at the bottom of the Cinder Depths, half-dreaming,
  leaking shard-fire into the world ever since. Everything twisted in the wilds
  is the Ember bleeding.

## Factions

- **Hearthwardens** — Meadowbrook's settler-protectors. Practical, kind,
  tired. They want the frontier to be *livable*, not glorious. Your first
  allies; the heart of the "cozy town." (Vendors, trainers, the starter arc.)
- **The Lantern Order** — scholars and relic-hunters, the Accord's
  half-ashamed inheritors. They preserve pre-Fall knowledge and license
  reclaimers. They know more about the Fall than they admit. (Quest-givers;
  the breadcrumb that pulls you deeper.)
- **The Cinderborn** — people the ember-scars *changed* rather than killed.
  Faintly luminous, long-lived, distrusted by the towns; they live closest to
  the Ashreach and hear the buried Ember "dreaming." Not villains — witnesses.
- **The Ashreach warlords** — whoever is currently strong enough to hold the
  richest ember-scarred ground. Human greed, not monsters; the reason the
  risk-zone is contested. (PvP / opt-in danger fiction.)
- **The Lampwrights' shadow** — the binding-keepers were thought extinct. A few
  of their successors survived, still convinced the answer to a broken fire is
  to bind it *harder*. The spine's true antagonists — ideologues, not demons.

## The bestiary, in-fiction

- **Emberlings** — small creatures kindled by shard-fire; the wilds' "wolves."
- **Ash Wolves** — predators that den in burned ground, drawn to warmth.
- **Bandits** — desperate frontier folk; the most human threat.
- **Ruin Sentinels** (later) — pre-Fall constructs still guarding Accord ruins,
  running a hundred-year-old order no one remembers giving.

## The Main Story spine (act by zone)

A breadcrumb structure (WoW flow, RS soul): each zone is an act, ending in a
**spotlight quest** (handcrafted set piece). Solo-completable; better with
friends. The five GDD zones map to five acts.

### Act I — Meadowbrook (the town, safe)
**"A roof and a reason."** You arrive as a homesteader with nothing. The
Hearthwardens take you in; you learn the world by helping it (the tutorial-as-
quests first 10 minutes). A Lantern Order courier dies at the gate clutching a
warm relic shard he shouldn't have, whispering a name — *Ashreach*. The
Hearthwardens want it buried and forgotten. The Order wants it studied.
**Spotlight:** *The Courier's Last Mile* — decide who you hand the shard to.
That choice colors who trusts you later.

### Act II — Greenreach (level 1–20 fields)
**"The frontier bites back."** Out in the fields you make your name: clear
camps, protect homesteads, prove the wilds can be pushed back. You notice the
ember-scars are *growing* — slowly, but toward something. The Lantern Order
recruits you to map the spread; the Cinderborn warn you to stop poking it.
**Spotlight:** *What the Scars Want* — track the spread to a buried Accord
lamp-line still pulling Ember underground a century later. Someone is keeping
the old machine running.

### Act III — Tanglewood (level 20–40 forest)
**"The forest remembers."** An overgrown Accord province where Ruin Sentinels
still patrol and the trees have drunk shard-fire for a century. Here you find
the Order's secret: pre-Fall records proving the Fall was *engineered*, and the
first hard evidence the Lampwrights survived. You also meet a Cinderborn elder
who can *hear* the buried Ember — and is terrified of what it's starting to
want. **Spotlight:** *The Lampwrights' Ledger* — recover the binding-plans;
learn the heart of the Ember is alive, wounded, and reachable through the
Cinder Depths.

### Act IV — The Ashreach (40+, risk zone)
**"Everything worth having is on fire."** The richest, most dangerous ground —
where shards fell thickest and warlords hold what they can take. Opt-in PvP and
the best drops live here *because* the fiction earns it: this is the approach to
the wound in the world. To reach the Depths you must cross contested ember-scar
and decide what you'll risk to do it. **Spotlight:** *The Long Burn* — broker,
fight, or outlast your way to the sealed Accord gate above the Cinder Depths.

### Act V — Cinder Depths (instanced dungeon, 3–5 players)
**"What's still dreaming down there."** The buried heart of the Ember, and the
last Lampwrights tending their hundred-year-old binding — convinced that if
they just pull *harder*, they can finish the Accord's dream and have an
everlasting flame at last. Three bosses = three stages of the wound (the
warden-construct, the shard-storm, and a Lampwright keeper). At the bottom: the
Ember itself, half-alive, and the choice the whole spine has been pointing at.

## The central choice (v1.0 climax, expandable post-1.0)

The Ember can't be "won." It can be:
- **Released** — break the century-old binding and let the wounded fire return
  to the world as it was: warmth and wild growth, but the ember-scars (and the
  danger, and the relics) bleed back into everything. A living, unsafe world.
- **Bound** — finish the Lampwrights' work and cage the Ember for good: the
  scars cool, the wilds calm, the frontier finally becomes *safe* — and a
  little colder, a little smaller, the magic going out of the world.
- **(Post-1.0) Tended** — the Cinderborn's heresy: neither cage nor unleash,
  but *keep* it — a slow, communal stewardship. The hardest path; the seed for
  later seasons.

There is no clean win, only what kind of world the server decides to live in.
The choice is framed as the player's, but its weight is meant to be a
*community* conversation — fitting "one world, one economy, one reputation."

## Themes

- **Appetite vs. stewardship.** The Fall wasn't evil magic; it was using a good
  thing too hard. Every economy/skilling sink in the game quietly echoes it.
- **The frontier is people, not monsters.** Bandits, warlords, and Lampwrights
  are all human. The scariest thing in Vesper is a reasonable person who is
  sure they're right.
- **Reputation is real.** A century after institutions died, what you're
  *known for* is the only law left. (Ties directly to the hiscores pillar.)

## How systems carry the story (so narrative ≠ cutscenes)

- **Skilling** = rebuilding the frontier with your hands; bronze→steel mirrors
  the world relighting itself without the Ember.
- **Relics (Rare/Relic tier)** = literal shards of the Accord's hubris; finding
  one is server news *and* a piece of lore.
- **The Ashreach risk zone** = the story's stakes made mechanical: the closer to
  the wound, the more you can gain and lose.
- **The economy ledger** = the Accord fell from unaccounted overdraw; the game
  refuses to repeat it (kit rule #6, only half-joking).
